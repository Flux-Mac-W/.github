## 📖 Overview

<a href="#flux">Flux</a> is a display color temperature management utility that automatically adjusts the Mac screen's color tone throughout the day — shifting from bright, blue-toned daylight display during the day to warm, amber-toned display after sunset — reducing the blue light exposure that disrupts circadian rhythm and makes evening screen use uncomfortable for the eyes.

The <a href="#flux">location-based automatic adjustment</a> uses the Mac's location to determine the precise local sunset and sunrise times — automatically beginning the color temperature shift toward warmer tones as sunset approaches and returning to daylight display color at sunrise. The transition is gradual and continuous rather than a sudden shift, making the change imperceptible in the moment while still completing the full adjustment from daylight to warm evening tones over the transition period. The <a href="#flux">color temperature range</a> spans from 6500K daylight equivalent through warm amber tones down to candlelight warmth at 1900K — users choose how warm the nighttime display becomes based on their preference and the level of blue light reduction they want. Higher warmth settings reduce blue light more aggressively; lower settings provide a subtle evening adjustment.

The <a href="#flux">sleep quality improvement rationale</a> is the core scientific basis for Flux — blue light from screens at night suppresses melatonin production and delays sleep onset by signaling to the brain that it is still daytime. By removing blue wavelengths from the display after sunset, Flux reduces this physiological signal and supports the natural transition to sleepiness that the circadian system produces as the evening progresses. The <a href="#flux">smooth transition timing</a> configures how quickly the color temperature changes — fast transitions for users who want the warm display immediately at sunset, very slow hour-long transitions for users who want the adjustment to be completely imperceptible.</p>

<a href="#flux">Movie mode</a> temporarily disables the color warming for accurate color rendering when watching films — a single click returns to full display color fidelity for the duration that matters, automatically returning to the warm setting afterward. <a href="#flux">Darkroom mode</a> inverts the display to a red-on-black color scheme for extreme low-light conditions — preserving night vision when working in very dark environments where even a warm amber display is too bright.

<a href="#flux">Candle and ember presets</a> provide very warm display settings at the lower end of the color temperature range — 1900K candle warmth and 2300K ember warmth for the most aggressive blue light reduction before sleep. <a href="#flux">Disable for specific applications</a> pauses the color adjustment when designated applications are in focus — color-sensitive creative work in photo editors and design tools can be configured to temporarily disable Flux while that application is active.</p>

## 💬 FAQ

<details>
<summary>What does Flux do to my display?</summary>

Flux gradually shifts the display color temperature from daylight white (6500K) toward warm amber tones (down to 1900K) after sunset, reducing blue light from the screen in the evening hours.

</details>

<details>
<summary>How does Flux know when sunset is?</summary>

Flux uses your Mac's location — either detected automatically or entered manually — to calculate the precise local sunset and sunrise times each day.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Does Flux affect display color accuracy?</summary>

Yes. The color warming shifts display colors toward amber, making the display unsuitable for color-accurate photo editing or design work when active. Use Movie Mode or app-specific disabling for color-critical applications.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Is Flux free?</summary>

Yes. Flux is completely free with no purchase, subscription, or premium tier.

</details>

<details>
<summary>How is Flux different from Apple's Night Shift?</summary>

Flux offers a wider color temperature range reaching 1900K candlelight warmth, more control over transition speed, application-specific disabling, and additional modes like Darkroom and Movie Mode that Night Shift doesn't provide.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Does Flux work with multiple monitors?</summary>

Yes. All connected displays adjust simultaneously for a consistent color environment across a multi-display setup.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Can I disable Flux temporarily?</summary>

Yes. Flux can be disabled for a set period — one hour, until sunrise — or permanently for specific applications.

</details>

<details>
<summary>What is Darkroom mode?</summary>

Darkroom mode inverts the display to a red-on-black color scheme for working in very dark environments where even warm amber display is too bright for night vision preservation.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Does Flux affect sleep quality?</summary>

Research on blue light and melatonin suppression supports the sleep quality benefit of reduced blue light exposure in the evening. Many users report improved sleep ease after using Flux consistently.

</details>
